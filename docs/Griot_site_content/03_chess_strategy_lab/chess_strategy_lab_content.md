# Chess Strategy Lab Content

## Page Purpose

The Chess Strategy Lab page should help parents understand what the chess program is, who it is for, what students practice, and why chess is a strong starting point for structured K-8 enrichment.

This page should not include detailed curriculum, weekly lesson plans, assignments, or assessment content. It should focus on parent decision-making and trial class conversion.

## Target Audience

Primary audience: parents and guardians of K-8 students who want their child to build focus, patience, planning, and problem-solving through chess.

Secondary audience: homeschool families, after-school program organizers, and school leaders interested in small-group chess enrichment.

## Conversion Goal

Primary conversion: Book a Trial Class.

Secondary conversion: Join Founding Families.

Support conversions: View Programs, Get Class Updates, Request School Demo, Contact Us.

## 3. What Students Practice Section

### Section Purpose

Translate chess into specific skills parents value without turning the page into curriculum.

### Headline

What students practice in Chess Strategy Lab.

### Subheadline

The goal is not just to learn moves. The goal is to practice better thinking habits.

### Body Copy

Students practice how to look at a position, notice patterns, compare options, and explain why a move makes sense.

Core practice areas include:

- Focus: pausing before making a move
- Planning: thinking one or more steps ahead
- Decision-making: comparing choices before acting
- Pattern recognition: noticing threats, opportunities, and repeated ideas
- Reflection: learning from mistakes without getting stuck
- Communication: explaining a move in clear language

## 4. Grade Bands Section

### Section Purpose

Show how the chess experience should adapt for different K-8 stages.

### Headline

Chess instruction should match the student's age and experience.

### Subheadline

Younger students need simple patterns and encouragement. Older students can handle deeper analysis and more independence.

### Body Copy

### K-2

Students are introduced to the board, pieces, simple vocabulary, turn-taking, and basic logic through short guided challenges.

### Grades 3-5

Students begin building stronger habits around planning, recognizing patterns, explaining choices, and learning from game positions.

### Grades 6-8

Students can work through more complex positions, compare candidate moves, discuss strategy, and build more independent study habits.

Final placement should consider both grade level and chess experience.
