# Chess Strategy Lab Image Direction

## Overall Standard

Images for Chess Strategy Lab should feel focused, calm, age-appropriate, and parent-trustworthy. The page should show chess as guided thinking practice, not an intimidating tournament scene.

Use real or approved photos whenever possible. Students should look K-8 age appropriate, and the chessboard should be visible enough for parents to understand the activity.

Avoid dark dramatic chess imagery, adult-only chess photos, university club scenes, overly competitive tournament halls, and generic stock images where students only smile at the camera.

---

## 1. Hero Image

- Image purpose: Show Chess Strategy Lab as guided K-8 enrichment.
- Recommended subject: Student and instructor working through a chess position at a table, with the board clearly visible.
- Emotional tone: Calm, focused, welcoming.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Student planning a chess move with instructor guidance"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: Adult-only chess, dark dramatic board close-ups, crowded tournament rooms, students who look older than K-8.

## 2. Parent Fit Section

- Image purpose: Help parents picture a child who is learning focus and planning.
- Recommended subject: Child considering a move, with a peer or instructor nearby.
- Emotional tone: Patient, attentive, supportive.
- Image type: Photo or small supporting thumbnail.
- Alt text suggestion: "Child thinking through a chess move during class"
- Launch priority: Can wait if hero image is strong.
- Images to avoid: Child looking frustrated, isolated, or pressured.

## 3. What Students Practice Section

- Image purpose: Connect chess to focus, planning, decision-making, and reflection.
- Recommended subject: Close but natural image of hands near a chessboard, with students discussing possible moves.
- Emotional tone: Thoughtful, collaborative.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Students comparing chess moves during a strategy activity"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: Generic chess piece macro photos with no students.

## 4. Grade Bands Section

- Image purpose: Show that chess can be adapted for different ages.
- Recommended subject: Three small visuals: younger student learning pieces, elementary students playing guided positions, middle grade students analyzing a board.
- Emotional tone: Clear, age-appropriate, organized.
- Image type: Photo set or simple icon-supported cards.
- Alt text suggestion: "Chess activities for K-8 grade bands"
- Launch priority: Can wait if grade-band copy is clear.
- Images to avoid: High school or college chess club imagery.

## 5. Beginner-Friendly Section

- Image purpose: Reduce intimidation for new chess families.
- Recommended subject: Instructor pointing to pieces or helping a beginner understand the board.
- Emotional tone: Encouraging, patient, approachable.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Instructor helping a beginner chess student"
- Launch priority: Required if beginner-friendly messaging is a major section.
- Images to avoid: Advanced notation-heavy visuals, serious tournament clocks as the main image.

## 6. How Class Works Section

- Image purpose: Show the guided class format.
- Recommended subject: Small group around a chessboard with instructor guidance.
- Emotional tone: Structured, collaborative, focused.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Small group chess class with instructor guidance"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: Large crowded rooms where individual guidance is not visible.

## 7. Parent Visibility Section

- Image purpose: Show how parents can follow program activity.
- Recommended subject: Parent dashboard screenshot or approved mockup showing enrolled chess class, upcoming session, progress, lesson activity, or messages.
- Emotional tone: Organized, clear, calm.
- Image type: Dashboard screenshot or UI mockup.
- Alt text suggestion: "Parent dashboard showing Chess Strategy Lab progress"
- Launch priority: Required if dashboard visibility is a major claim.
- Images to avoid: Screenshots with private student data, dense corporate analytics visuals.

## 8. Trial Class Section

- Image purpose: Make trial class feel approachable.
- Recommended subject: Student participating in a chess activity with teacher support.
- Emotional tone: Low-pressure, focused, encouraging.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Student participating in a guided chess trial class"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: Sales imagery, empty calendar visuals, generic signup graphics.

## 9. Founding Families Section

- Image purpose: Show early cohort participation without exaggerating scarcity.
- Recommended subject: Small chess group learning together or parent and child reviewing a class option.
- Emotional tone: Welcoming, organized, grounded.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Small group of students in a chess enrichment class"
- Launch priority: Can wait if trial and hero images are strong.
- Images to avoid: Celebration imagery that feels staged or unrelated to chess.

## 10. School and Group Programs Section

- Image purpose: Support school and group inquiry.
- Recommended subject: Chess club or after-school group with a teacher guiding students.
- Emotional tone: Credible, community-centered, structured.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Students participating in an after-school chess program"
- Launch priority: Can wait unless school chess outreach is a launch priority.
- Images to avoid: University tournament rooms, adult chess clubs, empty classrooms.

## Image Notes Before Publishing

- Use consistent card crops across the page.
- Keep the chessboard visible in key images.
- Show students thinking, discussing, or receiving guidance.
- Remove any image that feels too intense, too old, too staged, or too generic.
- Do not use chess imagery that implies tournament training unless that offering is confirmed.
