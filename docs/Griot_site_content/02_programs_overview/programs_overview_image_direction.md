# Programs Overview Image Direction

## Overall Standard

Images on the Programs Overview page should feel K-8 appropriate, warm, real, calm, focused, diverse, and parent-trustworthy. They should help parents understand what each program looks like in practice.

Avoid older teens, generic laptop photos, corporate software visuals, scary AI or robot imagery, university-style imagery, and daycare-style imagery.

---

## 1. Hero Image

- Image purpose: Show that the page is about choosing a practical enrichment path.
- Recommended subject: K-8 students working with an instructor in a small-group learning setting, with visible program materials such as a chessboard, laptop project, robotics parts, or math activity.
- Emotional tone: Calm, focused, organized, welcoming.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "K-8 students working with an instructor during an enrichment class"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: Generic classroom stock photos, older teens, large lecture rooms, abstract technology graphics.

## 2. Program Comparison Section

- Image purpose: Support parent decision-making without overwhelming the page.
- Recommended subject: A clean visual comparison block using simple icons or small thumbnails for chess, coding, robotics, math, and AI literacy.
- Emotional tone: Organized, practical, easy to scan.
- Image type: Icon set, small card thumbnails, or light UI graphic.
- Alt text suggestion: "Comparison of Griot Academy program areas"
- Launch priority: Can wait if program card images are strong.
- Images to avoid: Dense tables as images, decorative graphics with no learning context, corporate dashboard visuals.

## 3. Chess Card

- Image purpose: Show focus, strategy, and thoughtful decision-making.
- Recommended subject: A student and instructor working through a chess position with the board visible.
- Emotional tone: Focused, calm, attentive.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Student planning a chess move during a strategy lesson"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: Adult-only chess photos, tournament halls, dramatic dark chess images, students looking isolated or confused.

## 4. Coding Card

- Image purpose: Show coding as guided creation.
- Recommended subject: Student building a simple project on a laptop with an instructor or peer nearby.
- Emotional tone: Curious, supported, creative.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Student building a coding project with guidance"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: Generic laptop close-ups, adult developer scenes, screens filled with complex adult code, students who look older than K-8.

## 5. Robotics Card

- Image purpose: Show hands-on building, testing, and revision.
- Recommended subject: Students assembling or testing a small robotics build with components visible.
- Emotional tone: Active, practical, collaborative.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Students testing a small robotics project"
- Launch priority: Required if Robotics Lab is promoted above the fold; can wait if it is waitlist-only.
- Images to avoid: Industrial robots, humanoid robots, cluttered tables where the build is unclear, scary robot imagery.

## 6. Math Card

- Image purpose: Present math as reasoning and confidence-building.
- Recommended subject: Students working through a puzzle, pattern activity, whiteboard problem, or guided reasoning challenge.
- Emotional tone: Encouraging, clear, focused.
- Image type: Photo or simple illustration.
- Alt text suggestion: "Students solving a math reasoning challenge together"
- Launch priority: Required before a dedicated Math Reasoning page goes live; can wait for waitlist launch.
- Images to avoid: Worksheet-only images, calculator stock photos, advanced equations, students looking frustrated alone.

## 7. AI Literacy Card

- Image purpose: Show AI Literacy as guided, responsible technology education.
- Recommended subject: Teacher-led discussion with students comparing examples, prompts, or simple technology outputs.
- Emotional tone: Safe, thoughtful, age-appropriate.
- Image type: Photo or simple UI mockup paired with a student learning image.
- Alt text suggestion: "Students learning about AI tools with teacher guidance"
- Launch priority: Required before a dedicated AI Literacy page goes live; can wait for early access launch.
- Images to avoid: Robot faces, glowing brains, hacker imagery, dystopian AI graphics, abstract blue-purple technology art.

## 8. Grade Bands Section

- Image purpose: Show that programs adapt to K-8 developmental stages.
- Recommended subject: Three small visuals for K-2 exploration, Grades 3-5 guided skill building, and Grades 6-8 applied problem-solving.
- Emotional tone: Organized, age-appropriate, reassuring.
- Image type: Icon set, small photos, or simple illustrations.
- Alt text suggestion: "Grade bands for K-8 enrichment programs"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: High school imagery, daycare imagery, generic school supply flat lays.

## 9. Parent Visibility Section

- Image purpose: Build trust by showing how parents can follow progress.
- Recommended subject: Parent dashboard screenshot or approved mockup showing child profile, enrolled programs, upcoming classes, progress, assignments, quizzes, certificates, or messages.
- Emotional tone: Clear, calm, organized.
- Image type: Dashboard screenshot or UI mockup.
- Alt text suggestion: "Parent dashboard showing enrolled programs and student progress"
- Launch priority: Required if dashboard visibility is a major claim.
- Images to avoid: Dense corporate analytics dashboards, fake charts with no context, screenshots with private student data.

## 10. Trial Class Section

- Image purpose: Make the trial step feel approachable and useful.
- Recommended subject: Student participating in a small guided class or parent and child reviewing a program option.
- Emotional tone: Low-pressure, friendly, confident.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Student participating in a guided trial class"
- Launch priority: Required.
- Images to avoid: Sales-call imagery, generic calendars, pressured enrollment visuals.

## 11. School / Group Programs Section

- Image purpose: Route school and group visitors into a credible institutional path.
- Recommended subject: Teacher leading a small group in an after-school, classroom, library, homeschool pod, or community program setting.
- Emotional tone: Organized, community-centered, credible.
- Image type: Photo.
- Alt text suggestion: "Students participating in a school enrichment program"
- Launch priority: Can wait if school programs are not a launch priority; required before a school demo campaign.
- Images to avoid: University lecture halls, corporate training rooms, empty classrooms, adults-only meetings.

## Image Notes Before Publishing

- Use real program images when possible.
- Crop consistently across program cards.
- Keep students, instructors, and learning materials visible.
- Do not use AI-generated or stock-style visuals that make the academy feel less credible.
- Remove or replace any image that makes the students look older than the K-8 audience.
